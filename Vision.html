What is tinkering?

The term tinkering was first used in 1300s to describe tinsmiths roaming around mending household gadgets. Lately, tinkering is interpreted as more as a perspective than a vocation.

  • Fooling around directly with phenomena, tools and materials

  • Thinking with your hands and learning through doing

  • Slowing down and getting curious about the mysteries of everyday stuff around whims, enjoyment, frustration, and ultimately about enquiry

  • Not following a step-by-step set of directions that leads to a tidy end result, instead, investigating different phenomena ay your own terms questioning your assumptions about the way something works

  • Making something which reveals itself on the go

Rethink! The Tinkering Lab at IIT Roorkee

The Tinkering Lab at IIT Roorkee aims to balance the structured knowledge transfer to students in an externally controlled environment (classroom/laboratory sessions with a fixed mandate, pre-set time line, under the pressure of evaluation/judgement by others) with a space/platform equipped with some of the most emergent tools and technologies, that is confined by:

  • no curriculum: the curriculum is what an individual’s imagination allows

  • no rigid time regulations: accessible 9 hours a day (11 noon to 8 evening)

  • no evaluation pressures: an individual is one’s own judge

  • no regulations: just responsible behavior

While the innovative and IPR-generating ideas/output from the Tinkering Lab would be an indicator of its success in medium and long term, its success on the path of innovation, in the short term, would be gauged in terms of students’ enhanced:

  • Sense of purpose

  • Engagement with new ideas and technology

  • New Insights and deeper understanding

  • Interactions among each other, as individuals and in groups

  • Collaboration, facilitating multi-disciplinary perspectives and approaches
